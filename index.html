This project compares ZIP-code rent with local income and a user-selected annual income. Instead of only asking where rent is expensive, it asks where rent becomes unaffordable relative to income.

How affordability is calculated

Monthly rent is compared to monthly income using the 30% affordability rule. If rent takes 30% or less of income, it is considered affordable. This project also calculates the annual income required to afford each ZIP code’s rent: monthly rent × 12 ÷ 0.30. When analyzing a family of four, we incorporate empirical baseline cost metrics from the uploaded EPI family budgets dataset.
